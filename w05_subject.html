Snoopy's Origins

Snoopy is the pet beagle of Charlie Brown. He has 7 siblings: Spike, Belle, Marbles, Olaf, Andy, and two other unnamed siblings. He is adopted by Charlie Brown in a farm from his previous owner Lila. He first appears in the October 4, 1950 comic. In the beginning of the comics, Snoopy acts like a dog and walks on all fours. As the comic goes on, he becomes his own character as acts more like a person and walks on two feet. He sleeps on top of his doghouse instead of inside and sleeps on his back.

Alter Egos of Snoopy

In addition to being Snoopy, he is also the Flying Ace. The Flying Ace is a World War 1 pilot who fights the Red Baron. Snoopy's doghouse is used as the Flying Ace's fighter jet. Snoopy also acts as Joe Cool, the popular mysterious college student who leans against walls and wears sunglasses. Joe Cool also has a theme song.

Snoopy and Friends

Snoopy's best friend is a yellow bird named Woodstock. Woodstock is first mentioned in the June 22, 1970 comic. His owner is Charlie Brown, one of the main characters of the Peanuts comic strip. Some other characters that Snoopy interacts with are Linus and Lucy van Pelt, Sally, Charlie Brown's little sister, Peppermint Patty, Marcy, Franklin, and Pigpen.
